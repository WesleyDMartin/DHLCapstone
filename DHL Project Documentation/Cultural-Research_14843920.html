Why is Culture Important

As per Leighâ€™s request, this project will require at least one culture to study, for the sake of testing, as well as a list of questions that can be answered for that culture.

The following list of questions has been created with the generic situation and experience in mind. The following questionnaire should be applicable in any cultural platform. Also, this questionnaire follows as though an individual is actually having a conversation with another person.

Question

Explanation

1

Hello! My name is Humaira and I would like to know about your culture if that is okay with you.

To start a conversation, we often introduce ourselves first. Also, it is always polite to ask permission before asking people about anything.

Targeting Bangladeshi, Indigenous, Mennonite and Trinidadian culture.

2

What nationality you are from?

Geographically, where is your country located?

This is to know where the person is from unless this information is already known.

3

What language or languages did you learn to speak when you were growing up? Is there any other languages that are largely spoken in your country?

One might grow up with learning more than one language. Also, some countries have quite a few languages that are spoken or used as official language nationwide. Such as India.

4

What language do you prefer to speak at home (if applicable) ?

5

What does your ethnic group is known as or What people from your country/culture is known as?

Example: People from Bangladesh is known as Bangladeshi or Bengali and Mennonite people are known as Mennonite, etc

6

How is your culture different from other culture? Or How is your culture similar to the other culture?

Rather than comparing ones culture directly with another, it is preferred to ask the differences first or what makes the culture stand out.

What makes Bangladeshi culture different than Canadian culture.

7

Do you still have family and friends back home? Are you still in touch with your friends? If So,

How important it is for you to stay in touch with your culture?

Assuming the person lives in Canada, therefore, it is safe to ask this question.

8

What is or are the most important thing about your culture. For an example, family, food, respect, holidays, customs, land mass etc.

Different culture has different thing that they value. Some culture is more focused on food than the culture itself and some focuses on religion.

9

Was it easy for you to fit into the Canadian culture?

Since Canadian culture is more modern and different than Bangladeshi culture, therefore, it might be difficult for an individual to adapting to this culture.

10

What is the main religion in your country? Is there any other religion that exist in your country beside the main religion?

If we are targeting one culture or country, religion becomes one of the important topic.

11

Do you actively practice or participate in your religion?

This might be a common question or a question that can be asked naturally. However, people might be sensitive to it.

12

How many cultural or religious festivals do you have? Is there any special one that you miss?

Different culture have different cultural festivals and religious festivals.

13

What kind of food do you guys eat? How is it similar or different than other cultures?

Food is a very common questions that gets asked a lot. Also, different culture has different food that might be similar to other cultural food.

14

If you could describe a typical family event, how would you describe it?

This is where we can show a video to provide a more personalized experience.

Show a video of a more personalized family experience

15

What is the typical houses look like in your country/culture?

Different countries have their own way of structuring houses. In Canada houses are made of wood, however, in Bangladesh, houses are made of concrete, brick, cement etc.

Show show typical houses in Bangladesh

16

What is your meal time is like? Do you have family dinner or it does not matter?

Most of the culture has (mandatory) family dinner. This is one of the thing that still exist in Bangladesh.

17

What does the typical school looks like? Do you have co-ed school or girls/boys separate school?

Bangladesh is one of the countries where there are separate school for boys and for girls. Some schools has early/morning shift for girls and day shift for boys.

Show video of school.

18

How people normally greet each other?

19

What is your national fruit? (if any)

In Bangladesh, there are national fruit, fish, bird etc.

Perhaps show a picture of the fruit and other things.

20

What does the country side in your country look like?

The country side in Bangladesh is very different than the city. depending on where you go, there are still houses that are made of clay/mud, tin and wood. Some places people still bathe in the ponds.

This is an opportunity to display the country side to provide more personalized experience.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UHQvAk-mj9A

21

What is the population like in your country?

Currently the population of Bangladesh is 164 million. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demographics_of_Bangladesh

A video can be shown how dense the country is. Here are few examples

  1. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FWffD16layY

  2. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Rtsav021Rs

22

What is the typical hospitals look like in your country?

Hospitals in Bangladesh is definitely different than hospitals here. There are some advanced hospitals. However, average people cannot afford to get treatment there.

Video can be attached here to provide more personalized experience. Here is an example of one of the Government hospital in Bangladesh.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=trMvMtHa99U
